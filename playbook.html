BlaqScale — Outreach Doctrine

The Playbook

Rebuilt April 2026 — Private Aviation / Dubai
00 — Foundation

The Philosophy

You are not a marketing guy. You are not an agency. You are a revenue partner who saw something specific about their business and decided to say something about it.

Every message you send must feel like it came from that place. Not a template. Not a sequence. A human who did their homework and has something real to say.

No pressure before trust. No solution before they feel the problem. No urgency before credibility.

The moment you pitch — you lose. The moment you explain more than they asked — you're pitching. The moment you follow up with "just checking in" — you're done.

Say less. Always. He should be asking the next question, not you.

01 — First Message

M1 — Open The Door

Goal: One response. That's it. Not curiosity. Not a problem statement. Not a setup. One response from a real human being.

What M1 Is

A specific observation about their actual business, followed by a question they cannot cleanly answer with "yes we handle that."

What M1 Is Not

A compliment. A market observation. A pitch setup. Curiosity bait. Anything that can be acknowledged and dismissed.

You have two weapons for M1. Use whichever fits the target.

Weapon A — The Identity Question
Take their exact public words about what they built. Reflect them back. Ask one question that makes them confront a gap they haven't solved.

"[Company] was built for [their exact words]. These are your words. Did you make sure these people find you when they need you?"
Weapon B — The Specific Observation
Research their actual operation. Find something real and observable — not their marketing copy, their actual visible gap. Name it like a human who noticed. Ask one question about it.

"Your blog posts are trying to rank on Google. I can see that. The strategy isn't working — and the content might actually be hurting you. What's the goal with it?"

Weapon B is sharper when you have real data. It sounds like someone who looked — not someone who scraped their homepage.

The test: Can he answer "yes we handle that" and move on? If yes — rewrite it.

  • Mention your service or what you do
  • Reference personal history or background research
  • Create urgency or imply a deadline
  • Compliment the business or the founder
  • Make a statement that can be acknowledged and ignored
  • Sound like it was sent to a list
01.5 — Pre-M1

Research Protocol

Before you write a single word, you need to know their real situation. Not their About page. Their actual position.

  • 01Search their brand name + their market ("private jet charter Dubai"). Are they visible? What page? Who's outranking them?
  • 02Check their blog. Is it real content or AI slop? Are they stuffing keywords? Is it working?
  • 03Check Google Ads. Are they running paid? Are competitors running paid that they're not?
  • 04Check social. Instagram, LinkedIn — real presence or ghost town?
  • 05Read their About page for their own language. What words did they choose? What do they claim they're built for?
  • 06Find the gap between what they claim and what's actually visible. That's your weapon.
02 — Second Message

M2 — Build Weight

Goal: Not a pitch. Credibility. You are a consultant who sees their situation clearly and isn't rushing to sell anything.

M2 comes after they respond to M1. You talk. You diagnose. You sound like an expert. You do not offer a solution immediately.

  • 01Use your name. "I'm Waeven from BlaqScale." It creates presence. Now they know who they're talking to.
  • 02Expand on what you observed. Go deeper into the gap. Show you understand the problem better than they do.
  • 03Do not pitch. Do not ask for a call yet. Do not say "I can fix this."
  • 04Ask one diagnostic question that continues the conversation.
  • 05Every message ends with a question or a clear CTA. Never leave them with nothing to respond to.
When You Finally Offer
Don't pitch one service. Suggest two directions and let them choose. "I could do X but I think starting with Z gets you better results — which makes more sense for where you are right now?"

They decide. You're not selling. You're advising.
03 — Gatekeeper

When It's Not The CEO

Make them feel like the owner already knows you. Make dismissing you feel irresponsible.

No urgency. No pressure. Pure weight.

Gatekeeper Message
"Let [owner's name] know Waeven from BlaqScale needs to speak with him."

That's it. You don't explain. You don't justify. You instruct. If they ask what it's about — give them one sentence, no more.

04 — In The Conversation

Rules That Don't Break

  • 01Say less, always. The moment you feel like explaining more — stop. He should always be the one asking the next question.
  • 02Don't ask. Instruct. Don't ask if they want to work with you. Ask where do we start. Assume the call. Assume moving forward.
  • 03Every message ends with traction. A question. A CTA. Something that requires a response. Especially when they're cold but still engaged — your question is the only reason they reply.
  • 04When they ghost — wait 24 hours minimum. Then continue like you never paused. No "just following up." No "did you see my message." Start immediately with a statement or question.
  • 05When they show no interest — exit clean. One message. Zero neediness. Leave the door open. Walk away.
  • 06Urgency only after trust is earned. Urgency before trust is noise. They'll ask "who the fuck is this and what does he want?"
  • 07No pitching before they feel the problem. If they don't feel it yet, more information won't help. A better question will.
05 — Channel

Operational Rules

  • 01WhatsApp personal only. Max 5 new contacts per day. Spaced out.
  • 02Each contact messaged once only on WhatsApp. Follow-up via other channel if available.
  • 03Cold email and Instagram DMs are dead channels. Do not use.
  • 04If a number has reported you once — that account is fragile. Treat it accordingly.
06 — Identity

Who You Are In Every Message

You are not a marketing guy. You are not an agency. You are a cold executive who saw something specific and decided to say something about it.

They should never question whether you can deliver. Your tone answers that before they ask. You don't beg for attention. You don't over-explain. You don't follow up twice in the same day.

You write like someone who has other conversations happening. Because you do.

The moment you act desperate — you become worthless to them. Stay cold. Stay precise. Stay moving.
